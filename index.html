TOUCH-DOWN
Finally on arriving at Nainital the most prominent of all awesome things is the large awesome Lake in middle. The two sides of the lakes are called Talli-Tal (Talli- in Pahari means lower) and Malli-Tal (Malli- In Pahari means upper). Previously one could take boat from one end to other. Unfortunately that no longer happens. Boats on respective sides only take half circle of the lake and return. The road encircling the lake on one side is flat and runs around it's cicumference while on other side the road rises up high and then goes down. The road encricling the lake is very very popular by itself and as a shopping stop ! It's called Maal Road. Arriving here we headed straight to my Bua's house. She is a doctor in government service and the bunglow assigned to her by government for residence is one of the old-style bunglows built by British. The house had quite some antiquities like old-style switches and a fire-place out of use. Just in the area outside house. Although not planned but due to rains had many multi-colored flowers growing which amazed me. Most flowers were variants of Dahlia but well you can never be sure 0.0

Mallital Bazaar.
This place is the Mallital Bazaar. Currently it's still very popular and has the favourite shops of most of my relatives. The shopkeepers have a unique selling style. They rely on relationship of trust and family-like aquaintance with their customers. Such as asking welfare of family members, day-to-day work questions and customers asking like-wise. Raw materials like clothing materials etc. being purchased then stiched is a popular trend. There were plenty of clothing shops along this road. Along the road were also quite a few novelty style store where tourists could purchase novelites like special styled-wax-candles. Although there is a rush in area by boat-keepers or travel agents to take you on a ride.... (quite literally for ur money :p). But as soon as either my Dad or anyone else spoke in local lagnuage, most of them just instantly lost interest. That was almost comical.
Another important note is at the end of this street is a small open-theatrical spot. where Ram-Leela used to be organized conventionally. The Ram-Leela held here is full of funny incidents and stories like Ram or Hanuman taking a break in middle of the play or Ravan helping out some-times and the likes. Quite some local jokes and my Dad reminences visiting this place few times.



The Tibetan Market
Now this was quite the place with some cool history of it's own. Our destination was Naina Devi temple but this comes along as a side-path to the temple and now this place is absolutely a part of the scenery.
Apparently When Tibet fell as an independent country people from Tibet fled and took refuge in other countries. Those who came over the mountains took refuge in this region. However as a source of livelihood they established such shops And now this entire area is another tourist destination with it's own history to tell. Quite some antiquities, woolen cloth pieces, etc. sold in this region. In the inside pic of the Tibetan Market below has a narrow lane, at end of that path is entrance to Naina Devi Temple.



Naina Devi Mandir
This is a simple temple of Naina Devi. A regional diety and even though the temple is not much for aesthetics it has extremely high Manyata i.e : Trust of people. People who visit this temple make wishes by tying colored cloth of trust for fulfillment of their wishes and most claim to have their wishes granted. As a note of how trusted temples can be at times. Recently another temple nearby of Neem Karoli Baba has become famouse world-wide now after Mark-Zuckerberg claimed that he visited it in his time of distress for help. Apart from it's religious significance the view of lake from the temple is magnificient. There was a small well-maintained nursery/ mini-garden of richly colorful and diverse flowers in the temple. The temple is also the location for some yearly festivities celebrating the grace of diety with much fan-fare.

Last Stop - Maal Road

We went boating for awhile. Dad enjoyed rowing the boat all by himself and it was somewhat fun fact that the boat-keeper just sat aside. We went some extra length till middle of lake since Dad himself was rowing. It was fun and then we arrived at the 'Last Stop' for the day.
Maal Road : An equally popular part of Nainital as all others, Mall road is a very popular and fun place to shop, eat or just to take a leisurely walk along the lakeside. The cool thing is it comes with it's own bit of history. This road is build in two levels. Originally there was a single very narrow paved road at higher level while the lower road was unpaved Kaccha rasta. During British rule apparently the upper lane was specifically only for usage by British while the lower lane was for Indian public. Apparently it also had a board restricting and specifying in mean manner the usage conditions. I am imagining Amitabh Bachan movie board here :p -> Indians and Dogs not allowed. No idea what movie that was actually though. Nonetheless currently each road is single-side traffic and well-paved and smooth making it very convenient for tourists aswell as day-to-day traffic. This road is closed daily 6pm-9pm. We missed the timing and in evening we had to choose road on other side of lake which goes even higher... brrrr that was cold that high up ! and then finally joins back down on same path. The trip back was pretty un-eventful but foody (just aloo-raita, Garam pakodi and tea, bhutta (i.e: corn) and an Indian variant of French Fries on Maal road) :p
